The central question I would like to ask is: Whose body is performing the script? I want to explain this with a bit more context but I also want to mention that I am still in the early stages of my thesis, so some parts may be undeveloped and unclear. My inquiry relates to the previously described technological conditions shaping the relationship between humans (or users) and machines—and how those conditions influence behavior. In this question, I use „body“ as a non-specific term to refer to the user, while also opening up two lines of interpretation. First, it could relate to the identity of WHO is the person: is the user the SAME as the performer? Second, it can be understood in a possessive sense: to WHOM does the body belong that is performing the script? Is it the user’s OWN body performing, or is it the choreographer’s vision/intent coming through them?Ultimately, this raises broader questions around agency and influence, authorship and autonomy. [Press ->]I would like to draw a conceptual comparison with characteristics of performative practices in theatre plays, where performances are considered as a laid-out instruction scripts and are carried out as a twice behaved or stored, recalled and embodied behaviour. These are executed by a body of a performer who behave as someone else, as another version of „me“. [Press ->] With this thesis, I am proposing that in the relationship between humans and machines, users are performing actors – playing roles dictated by a screenplay script of the programmer, who acts as a kind of dramaturg. In other words computation determines which role we are playing and how we “behave.” Building on this analogy, I would like to explore how we might resist/refuse or escape this rigid environment in order to gain agency by asking: Does the abstractness of a software script allow for misinterpretation? Where do moments of impulsiveness, improvisation or spontaneity emerge (off-script and free-style!)? Who is the audience? Who is the protagonist and who is the supporting cast? Is there a possibility of collaboration between user-performer and programmer-dramaturg? Who is the voice inside the prompter? And Who is the stunt double?
